What does Pets Haven Foundation do?

Pets Haven is not your normal everyday animal shelter. We are a Pro-Life facility, we do not believe in euthanizing healthy animals for economic reasons or to simply make room. Our emphasis relates to helping both human beings and our orphaned animals. We strongly believe in respecting both ‘human and fur’. Our aim is to responsibly rehouse companion pets back into our community, where they belong. We rescue animals from pounds, puppy farms, cruelty cases and take in animals from members of the community, who can no longer look after their pets. All of our orphans are provided with the appropriate veterinary treatment such as health checks, desexing, vaccinations, micro chipping, worming, flea treatments, dentals and we even parvovirus test our canines. Pets Haven Foundation is not funded in any way. We solely rely on donations and fundraising events.

How your donations help?

Pets Haven rely solely on donations and fundraising to keep the shelter running. Here are a few things your donation will assist with: Rent, Electricity, Water, Insurance, Vehicle Registration/Insurance and Maintenance, Petrol, Veterinary Fees, Insurance Fees, Staff wages, Internet, Food, Medications, Flea and Worm Treatment, Grooming, Building maintenance, Office supplies, Cleaning supplies, Kitty Litter and Hay and Veterinary treatment for our rescue horses.

Share in the success of Pets Haven Foundation?

Pets Haven rescue a lot of animals involved in cruelty cases, such as Stella. Stella is a 10 year old German Shepherd who was left tied to a stable door with no food and little water until she was on deaths door. When she came into Pets Haven's care, she could barely stand, she was severely emaciated and dehydrated and required immediate lifesaving fluids, antibiotics and hand feeding of special no fat food, as her gut could not handle anything rich. Stella's chain had severely burnt her neck in a particularly hot summer week we were having and she was covered in pustules and tumours. Stella, 8 months on, is still in Pets Havens care. She has had thousands of dollars in blood tests, specialist visits, veterinary visits, special dietary food and treatments. Stella is still not strong enough to have her mammary tumours surgically removed so will live the remainder of her life in foster care under Pets Haven's staff and vets guidance.
